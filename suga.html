In the year 2013, he debuted as a member of BTS, the famous South Korean pop idol group. This further gained him a lot of fame. 

Suga Wiki/Biography

Born on 09 March 1993, Suga’s age is 28 years as of April 2021. He was born and brought up in a middle-class Christian family from Daegu, South Korea. He is South Korean by nationality, and he does not have any religion.

He completed his early schooling at the Taejeon Elementary School, Apgujeong High School and later in the Gwaneum Middle School in South Korea. 

He later enrolled himself at the Hanyang University in South Korea from where he completed his graduation. From the beginning of his childhood, he was more interested in singing. He also used to participate in many co-curricular activities in his school days.

Career

Suga started his career by joining a company as a music producer. In the year 2010, he produced the song titled ‘518-062’. In the year 2012, he released the song titled ‘All I Do Is Win’.

In the year 2013, he released the songs titled ‘Dream Money’, ‘Adult Child’, ‘’It Doesn’t Matter’’. In the year 2015, he released the charted song titled ‘Intro: The Most Beautiful Moment in Life’. 

In the year 2016, he released the mixtape titled ‘August D’. This year he also released the charted songs titled ‘Give it to Me’, ‘The Last’, ‘So Far Away, ‘Tony Montana’, and ‘First Love’. 

In the year 2017, he produced the song titled ‘Wine’.

In the year 2018, he released the charted song titled ‘Trivia: Seesaw’. This year he released the song titled ‘Ddaeng’. 

In the year 2019, he released the charted song as a featured artist titled ‘Song Request’, and ‘’Suga’s Interlude’’. This year he also produced the songs titled ‘Eternal Sunshine, and ‘’We Don’t Talk Together’’.

Awards

Suga has won the 2017 Melon Music Hot Trend Award, 2019, and 2020 Mnet Asian Music Awards for Best Collaboration. Apart from these, along with the BTS band, he has been nominated for 536 awards, out of which he has won 361.

This includes awards from American Music Awards, Asian Artist Awards, Billboard Music Awards, Gennie Music Awards, iHeartRadio Music Awards, Japan Gold Disc Awards, Korean Music Awards, Melon Music Awards, MTV Awards, Nickelodeon Awards, NME Awards, NRJ Awards, Seoul Music Awards, Shorty Awards, Soompi Awards, Teen Choice Awards, and many more.
