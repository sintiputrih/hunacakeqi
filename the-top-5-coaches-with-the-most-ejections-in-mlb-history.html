The top 5 Coaches With the Most Ejections in MLB History

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Sunday, April 21, 2024

Major League Baseball has seen its fair share of colorful characters in managerial positions, some of whom have become notorious for their fiery temperaments and frequent ejections from games.

Let's delve into the ejection histories of the top 5 coaches with the most ejections in MLB history and explore the impact of their actions on their teams and the league.

1. Earl Weaver

Earl Weaver, known for his animated presence in the dugout, managed the Baltimore Orioles for 17 years. Despite his relatively short managerial career, Weaver's passionate nature led to numerous ejections throughout his tenure.

His career winning percentage of .583 ranks third among managers with at least 2,500 games managed. Weaver's ejections were often fueled by his intense desire to defend his players or dispute umpires' calls.

His impact on the Orioles was significant, leading the team to four AL pennants and securing the 1970 World Series title.

2. Lou Piniella

Lou Piniella, a prominent figure in the world of baseball, earned a reputation for his explosive outbursts during his managerial career.

His colorful language and heated confrontations with umpires often resulted in ejections from games.

Piniella's most memorable ejections include kicking dirt on umpires, throwing his hat on the ground, and verbally berating officials. 

3. Bobby Cox

Bobby Cox, renowned for his successful stint with the Atlanta Braves, holds a record for the most ejections by a manager in MLB history.

Cox's fiery demeanor and passionate defense of his players often led to confrontations with umpires, resulting in a remarkable 161 ejections during his career.

Despite his ejections, Cox's impact on the Braves was undeniable, leading the team to an unprecedented 15 consecutive division titles and a World Series victory in 1995.

4. Billy Martin

Billy Martin, a controversial and fiery manager, was known for his explosive temper and frequent ejections from games.

His confrontations with umpires often escalated to the point of physical altercations, making him a polarizing figure in the league.

Martin's most infamous meltdown involved him throwing dirt at an umpire and subsequently attempting to have the same umpire murdered.

His tumultuous relationship with umpires and his players created a volatile environment, leaving a lasting impact on the teams he managed.

5. Ozzie Guillen

Ozzie Guillen, known for his colorful personality, had a penchant for heated arguments with umpires that often led to his ejection from games.

His most notable ejection involved him kicking Geovany Soto's mask off the ground in a fit of rage.

Guillen's confrontational style and fiery demeanor brought both excitement and controversy to the teams he managed, making him a memorable figure in the history of MLB ejections.

 

These managers, known for their passionate and sometimes confrontational approach to the game, left an indelible mark on the league through their frequent ejections.

While their actions occasionally courted controversy, they undeniably added an extra layer of drama and excitement to the sport, making them unforgettable figures in the annals of MLB history.
