Lukas Forchhammer: biography, family, education

Lukas Graham Forchhammer was born on September 18, 1988 in Freetown Christiania, Denamrk. His mother worked as a night cleaner. His father, Eugene Graham, worked repairing and renovating old household items. However, his father died at the age of 61 due to a heart attack. He grew up in an anarchist community in Copenhagen, where he joined the Copenhagen Boys Choir at the age of 8.

From a very young age he was interested in classical music and also trained in it. He also once said that he became interested in classical music because of his father’s Irish roots. Before forming his band Lukas Graham in 2010, he spent six months in Buenos Aires, Argentina. During his childhood days, he appeared in the popular Danish film series ‘Krummerne’.

Lukas Forchhammer: career and achievements in life

As mentioned above, he started learning music from a young age. After years, in 2011, he formed a band with his other members: Magnus Larsson and Mark “Lovestick” Falgren. Also, his style of banda music is described as a pop-soul hybrid and some have described his music as where pop meets R&B. Also, he initially started uploading his songs to YouTube and Facebook before breaking into the mainstream.

His band signed a record deal with the Danish record label Copenhagen Records. They then first released a self-titled studio album in 2012, selling 80,000 albums and over 10,000 singles in Denmark during that year. They then toured Europe and other countries such as England, Sweden, Spain, Austria, and France. They gained international recognition in 2015 with songs like ‘Mama Said’, ‘7 Years’ and ‘Strip No More’ from the album ‘The Blue’. The band performed on the 17th season of American Idol in April 2019 during that second night of Top 20 Duets.
