What are the Lyrics to Ding Dong the Witch is Dead?

The lyrics to Ding Dong the Witch is Dead are a song from the 1939 film The Wizard of Oz, composed by Harold Arlen and written by E. Y. Harburg. The song is sung by the Munchkins, Glinda the Good Witch, and Dorothy Gale, to celebrate the death of the Wicked Witch of the East, who was crushed by Dorothy’s house. The song consists of several parts, each with a different melody and tempo. The song starts with Glinda encouraging the Munchkins to come out and meet Dorothy, who “fell from a star”. Then, the Munchkins sing the main chorus of “Ding Dong the Witch is Dead”, followed by a series of interludes, where the city officials verify the witch’s death, the Lullaby League and the Lollipop Guild welcome Dorothy, and the Munchkins praise her for killing the witch. The song ends with a reprise of the chorus and a final fanfare.

Why is Ding Dong the Witch is Dead Famous?

The song also showcases the talents of the actors, singers, and dancers who played the Munchkins, as well as the costumes, sets, and special effects that created the magical world of Oz. The song is also notable for its clever use of rhymes, wordplay, and alliteration, such as “witch”, “which”, “sitch”-uation, “rich”, and so on.
