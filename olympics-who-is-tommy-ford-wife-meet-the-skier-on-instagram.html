Olympics: Who Is Tommy Ford Wife? Meet The Skier On Instagram

Posted by Martina Birk on Tuesday, July 2, 2024

Tommy Ford is an American alpine ski racer who competes in the World Cup. Let us find out whether he has a wife or not and other personal details.

Tommy Ford specializes in giant slalom and super-G, with a first-place finish in a giant slalom event in December of 2019 being his greatest World Cup result to date.

Ford has competed in two Winter Olympics and four World Championships for the United States. Moreover, he skied and raced at neighboring Mount Bachelor before going on to Dartmouth College in New Hampshire, where he went on to win eight US Alpine Championship titles.

Olympics: Tommy Ford Wife – Is He Married?

Tommy Ford is unmarried as of 2022. However, Ford is dating his long-time girlfriend and former U.S. teammate, Laurenne Ross.

Laurenne Ross is an American alpine ski racer who competes on the World Cup circuit. She excels in downhill and super G speed races.

Moreover, Ross, who was born in Edmonton, Alberta, Canada, was on skis at the Snow Valley ski slope at the age of two, as her father was a former alpine racer.

In addition, in 2006, she was named to the United States Ski Team, and in December 2009, she made her World Cup debut.

Ross was promoted to the World Cup squad for the 2011 season and competed for the United States at the 2011 World Championships in the women’s downhill, where she finished tenth.

Ross finished second in downhill at Garmisch-Partenkirchen, Germany, in March 2013, for her first World Cup podium. In addition, she moved from Atomic to Völkl skis in the summer of 2013.

Ross splits her time between the professional ski circuit and her academics at the University of Oregon in Eugene.

Tommy Ford Parents – Who Are They?

Tommy Ford was born to his parents, Mark Ford and Mary Ellen, on March 20, 1989, in Hanover, New Hampshire.

His father, Mark, and his mother, Mary, were both ski racers and coaches. As a result, Ford began skiing when he was two years old, as both of his parents were racers and coaches.

Mary received her bachelor’s degree from Northern Vermont University in 1974 and was recognized in the university’s Hall of Fame in 2000.
