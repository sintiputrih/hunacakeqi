What Happened to OJ Mayo?

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Friday, October 4, 2024

OJ Mayo, a former NBA player, experienced a promising start to his career but eventually faced setbacks that led to his departure from the NBA. Below is an overview of Mayo's career, including his rise in the NBA, the issues that led to his ban, and his current status.

Promising Start and Early Career

OJ Mayo began his basketball journey by playing college basketball at the University of Southern California (USC). Following his college career, he declared for the 2008 NBA draft and was selected as the third overall pick by the Minnesota Timberwolves. Mayo's early years in the NBA were marked by high-level scoring, making significant contributions to the teams he played for, particularly the Memphis Grizzlies.

Setbacks and Bans

Mayo's career took a turn when he faced off-the-court issues, including a suspension in 2011 for testing positive for a banned substance, which he attributed to an over-the-counter supplement. This suspension was followed by a ban from the NBA for failing the anti-drug program. Despite applying for reinstatement in 2016 and getting approved, Mayo did not find a team willing to take a chance on him. Subsequently, his eligibility for reinstatement in the NBA ended without any team signing him, effectively ending his career in the United States.

Legacy and Current Status

Unfortunately, Mayo's legacy in the NBA has been overshadowed by the longest suspension in league history. Despite the setbacks, Mayo, now 33 years old, found an opportunity to continue his basketball career in the Chinese Basketball Association, playing for the Liaoning Flying Leopards.

OJ Mayo's journey in the NBA started with promise, but it was marred by off-the-court issues that led to his ban from the league. Although his NBA career faced an abrupt end, he found a new opportunity to continue playing in the Chinese Basketball Association. Mayo's story serves as a reminder of the challenges and consequences that professional athletes can face both on and off the court.
