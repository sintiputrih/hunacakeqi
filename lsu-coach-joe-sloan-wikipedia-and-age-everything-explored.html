Joe Sloan’s Impactful Journey

Louisiana Tech’s Offensive Maestro

In the dynamic realm of collegiate football, Joe Sloan stands as the linchpin of Louisiana Tech’s offensive prowess, entering his second season as the team’s Interim Head Coach and ninth overall season with the Bulldogs.1

With a multifaceted role that extends to overseeing the LA Tech quarterbacks, Sloan’s journey is marked by an impressive trajectory.

From Inside Receivers Coach to Interim Head Coach

Sloan’s odyssey in Tech coaching commenced in 2013 as the inside receivers coach, swiftly ascending to the role of recruiting coordinator in 2014. His unwavering commitment led to being named the assistant head coach in 2015.

The pinnacle of his coaching career was reached in 2020 when he earned the title of offensive coordinator under the leadership of Skip Holtz.

Recruiting Dynamo: A Force to Reckon With

Sloan’s impact extends beyond the field, spearheading recruiting efforts that culminated in the Bulldogs boasting the number one recruiting class in Conference USA in 2020. Rivals acknowledged this achievement by ranking the Bulldogs at an impressive fourth position among G5 programs.

Mastermind Behind Offensive Triumphs

During the 2020 campaign, Sloan orchestrated an offensive spectacle, guiding Louisiana Tech to rank first in C-USA and second in the FBS in fourth-down conversions at a staggering 88.9 percent.

The red zone offense showcased Sloan’s strategic prowess, securing a fourth-place ranking in C-USA and 53rd in the FBS at 84.6 percent.

Nurturing Talent: The Sloan Effect

Sloan’s coaching prowess was vividly evident in the development of quarterback Luke Anthony, who clinched the 2020 C-USA Newcomer of the Year title. Additionally, offensive linemen and receivers garnered conference honors, solidifying Sloan’s role in shaping top-tier offensive threats.

Bowl Dominance and Record-Breaking Seasons

A vital component of Louisiana Tech’s seven straight bowl appearances, Sloan played a pivotal role in the program’s historic 10-win season in 2019. The Bulldogs’ shutout victory over the Miami Hurricanes marked a milestone in Independence Bowl history.

Statistical Triumphs: Setting Benchmarks in C-USA

In 2019, Sloan’s guidance resulted in offensive prowess, with Louisiana Tech ranking second in scoring, total offense, and third-down conversion percentage within C-USA. This success continued in 2018, culminating in a decisive victory over Hawaii in the SoFi Hawaii Bowl.

Legacy of Excellence: Building on Past Glories

Sloan’s tenure saw LA Tech clinch a C-USA West Division title in 2016, showcasing the team’s dominance in completion percentage, first downs, and passing offense. Notably, Sloan played a key role in the Bulldogs’ fifth-most improved scoring offense in 2014, leading to a Heart of Dallas Bowl title.

Journey to Coaching Eminence

Sloan’s journey to coaching eminence began as a graduate assistant at South Florida, eventually joining the Louisiana Tech family. His extensive experience, both as a player and coach, underscores his deep connection with the Bulldogs.

A Scholar-Athlete’s Legacy

Beyond the gridiron, Sloan’s academic excellence shines. A Summa Cum Laude graduate in business administration, managerial finance from ECU, Sloan’s commitment to excellence extended to earning an MBA in May 2010.

His accolades include nominations for the Rudy Award and multiple recognitions for superior academic achievement.
