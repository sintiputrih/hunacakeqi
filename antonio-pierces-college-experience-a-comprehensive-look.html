Antonio Pierce's playing career is widely viewed in American football as one of the greatest careers a player, coach, and analyst could have. Indeed, his path from high school graduate to college student profoundly impacted the mold of Salcedo's life and career. Antonio Pierce Background Info

Antonio Durran Pierce, born October 26, 1978, is an American football coach and former linebacker. He had a very illustrious career in the National Football League (NFL), including playing for the Washington Redskins and New York Giants. Pierce was a member of the roster for the Giants' 2007 Super Bowl title. He later moved into coaching following his career and has held several different roles on coaches' staffs before being named the head coach of the Las Vegas Raiders. He also dabbled in sports commentary, serving as an NFL analyst for ESPN.

A bit of an overachieving student-athlete at the University of Arizona, Pierce was serious about his schoolwork and athletic endeavors.  Pierce put it all together on the field his senior season, recording three sacks, 77 tackles, ten for losses, two forced fumbles, one interception, and a blocked kick. His outstanding season earned him an All-Pac-10 honorable mention.

How College Shaped His Career

Pierce's college career at the University of Arizona helped get him on his way to future pursuits in football. He used his time at the university to develop and improve as a field athlete in high school, competing while he nurtured interests outside of athletics. Surely, the skills, discipline, and ability to see things ahead that he developed in college helped him both as a player and later on when he became a coach. His college career at the University of Arizona only served to prepare him for his professional football tenure.

Antonio Pierce's time at the University of Arizona defined, in part, his lifelong journey through American football. He was molded and trained into an upper-echelon athlete thanks to his time at the university, which led him to learn all he needed to succeed against professional competition.
